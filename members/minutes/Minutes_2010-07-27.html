Cabin managers report:


The batteries for the exit lights are not lasting long (2 years); Pete is getting some spares in-case of failure during the season. He is also investigating getting a live circuit so they run continuously, although the tubes last a shorter time, although this is cheaper than replacing batteries.


There was a burst pipe, as there was a cold snap early in the season before the heating had been turned on. Therefore the heater will be turned onto 8, and the water left on, from Queens Birthday weekend.


Leading on from this, as the nearest plumber is now in Bright, Pete is going to buy plumbers tools etc and leave them up there so that running repairs can be undertaken.


During the work party, they were going to paint roof and walls, but due to rain it didn’t occur. It should be noted that during the work party the Farrow girls did lots of painting.

General business


It was proposed to spend money on new sofas as outlined above (proposed by Sandy Clark, seconded by Bill Mitton), and passed



Sandy & Peri discussed the idea of redeveloping the site.


Lease expires 2017, so the question is wholesale redevelopment or less, but we have to do something. The first thing therefore needed is a business plan for the next 2-3 years.


To redevelop the site will cost estimates $300 – 500k. There is no possibility of bank funding, as they won’t loan without personal guarantees. Which therefore leads to the conclusion of members equity being the only way to achieve being able to obtain a new lease.


Ways of doing this are:


  • Members pay a straight levy; new members pay a building levy; and we use current funds

  • 2nd option – amend the club from being incorporated to corporated, with members holding transferable shares

    • Peter Twomey noted that this could cause tax issues, such as a tax on profits, and capital gains tax. He then suggested looking into a long tem debenture system

    • Peter Smith is going to put the legal advice obtained a few years ago about potential structures of the club on the website


It was decided not to pursue the full redevelopment of the site ($300-500k), as there would be virtually no members willing to pay a levy of $20,000. It was decided to further investigate the re-cladding option, and see what extension of the lease Hotham Management would give for it.



Therefore Hamish Farrow proposed we should commence the process, with funds, to get undertaking from Hotham Management to extend the lease for a re-cladding option in the order of $140k. This was seconded by Sandy Clark, and passed.
