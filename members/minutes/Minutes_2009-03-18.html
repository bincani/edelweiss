Previous minutes to be a true reflection: Proposed by Judy McCowan, seconded by Hamish


Business arising:

Redevelopment: Sandy and Peri have yet to complete the report on the redevelopment. They have said that it will be completed by the next meeting.


Jodie Farrow has visited a kit home factory, and will bring details to the AGM. Ingrid brought some information about designs back from Canada to pass around. It was noted that given the current financial crisis, there could be lots of tradespeople looking for work, making it potentially an advantageous time to build.


Fran is going ahead with the Edelweiss jumpers. She will contact people before winter to allow them to place orders.


The garbage hutch is moving along. There have been a couple of suggested designs given to the person who has made previous Mt Hotham hutches, and the price is decreasing. It was proposed by Judy that we give the go ahead once the price has dropped to an attractive price point, and this was seconded by Martin.


Correspondence

There has been a break down in communication with Mt Hotham in terms of fire preparation. Hamish received a letter from Mt Hotham saying they’d look into any problems with the cabin re: fire preparedness and get back to us. Unfortunately we Hamish recently found out that Greg O’Donohue, the fire officer, expected us to contact him. This was reinforced by Judy who, on a recent trip, was approached by Greg who was upset that the club didn’t get back to him. He also informed her that he was upset due to a throwaway remark a few years ago, and that wanted the cabin burnt down. Hamish will send a letter to Greg, to both clear up the confusion, and to enquire about any work that needs to be done around the cabin.


Hamish received a letter from the Hotham Ski Association, asking for membership levies of $50 each for ’07 and ’08. Hamish is writing to them to say that we have no record of us ever being a member, but also asking about what they are offering.


Cabin maintenance

Due to the missed work party due to the fires, a series of replacement actions has been organised:

  • Martin will be on the lookout for a cheap ‘low’ Dyson, and when one becomes available will purchase it. Proposed by Hamish, seconded by Judy

  • The electrician who has done work on the cabin before (mate of the Alpine Fire Extinguishers guy), is ok to fix the light in bedroom 2

  • Nancy and Judy will buy toiletries, and Andrew will take them up on the first weekend of the season

    • It has been proposed we make a list of the required quantities of toiletries/cleaning products, so that it is easier to do a pre-season buy

  • Kerry has provided the names of a couple of workers able to do clearing around the cabin/do work on the cabin. He says they are good, reliable, and provide all their own equipment. It has been proposed as an alternate way to do maintenance on/around the cabin – a couple of club members go up to organise and supervise, and the work party levy goes to paying them. It was thought it would be particularly useful if Mt Hotham required the clearing of vegetation around the cabin.

  • There was a question about whether the doonas were washed after the end of last season

  • Martin noted that the pink couches has passed their used by date. He is going to get measurements and look at costs for getting new ones.


On the 31st March, Hamish and Martin are going to the Hotham briefing, and will report back at AGM.
